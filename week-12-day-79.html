Requesting and Booking Services Clearly

Day 1 taught you to plan and coordinate group travel. Day 2 teaches you how to request and book services clearly — from booking hotels and restaurants to requesting special services. B2 speakers make vague requests. C1 speakers make clear, specific requests that get results.

Today's Philosophy

Requesting Services = Clarity + Specificity + Politeness

"Requesting services isn't about being polite. It's about being clear, specific, and polite. When you're clear about what you want, you get exactly what you want first time."

Every service request requires you to make five decisions simultaneously:

Clarity Decision: How clear am I about what I want? (Clear? Vague?)
Specificity Decision: How specific am I about details? (Specific? Vague?)
Politeness Decision: How polite am I? (Polite? Direct?)
Confirmation Decision: How do I confirm the booking? (Confirm? Assume?)
Modification Decision: How do I modify the booking? (Modify? Cancel?)

Important: Day 2 teaches you how to request and book services clearly. When you're clear, you get results.

The Service Booking Loop

The 3-step process for requesting and booking services clearly

1
REQUEST (Make your request)
How do I request? "I'd like to book..." / "I'd like to request..." (Make your request clearly)
2
SPECIFY (Specify the details)
How do I specify? "I'd like a double room with a sea view for three nights..." (Specify all details)
3
CONFIRM (Confirm the booking)
How do I confirm? "Could you confirm the booking details?" (Confirm all details)

5 Service Request Lenses (3-Level Examples)

Every service request requires five decisions. Each lens has 3 levels of examples: Simple → Natural → C1 Professional.

1

Clarity (How clear am I?)

"How clear am I about what I want?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Clarity Decision

3-Level Examples

Simple (B2) "I want to book a room."
Natural (B2+) "I'd like to book a double room for three nights, checking in on Friday."
C1 Professional "I'd like to book a double room with a sea view for three nights, checking in on Friday the 15th and checking out on Monday the 18th. Could you confirm the total price including all taxes?"

Life Example

Booking hotel. Decision: Clear request with all details ("I'd like to book a double room with a sea view for three nights...")

Business Example

Booking conference room. Decision: Clear request with all details ("I'd like to book the main conference room for 20 people on Friday...")

️ Trade-offs
Gain (Clear) Gets exactly what you want first time.
Lose (Clear) Takes more time to specify all details.

Common Mistake

Being vague ("I want to book a room") instead of clear ("I'd like to book a double room with a sea view for three nights...").

Reflection: Am I being clear about what I want?

2

Specificity (How specific am I?)

"How specific am I about details?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Specificity Decision

3-Level Examples

Simple (B2) "I want a room for a few nights."
Natural (B2+) "I'd like a double room for three nights, from Friday to Monday."
C1 Professional "I'd like to book a double room with a sea view for three nights, checking in on Friday the 15th at 3pm and checking out on Monday the 18th at 11am. Could you confirm the total price including breakfast and all taxes?"

Life Example

Booking hotel. Decision: Specific with all details ("I'd like a double room with a sea view for three nights, checking in on Friday...")

Business Example

Booking conference room. Decision: Specific with all details ("I'd like to book the main conference room for 20 people on Friday from 9am to 5pm...")

⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Specific) Gets exactly what you want, avoids confusion.
Lose (Specific) Takes more time to specify all details.

Common Mistake

Being vague ("I want a room for a few nights") instead of specific ("I'd like a double room for three nights, from Friday to Monday").

Reflection: Am I being specific about details?

3

Politeness (How polite am I?)

"How polite am I?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Politeness Decision

3-Level Examples

Simple (B2) "I want to book a room."
Natural (B2+) "I'd like to book a room, please."
C1 Professional "I'd like to book a double room with a sea view for three nights, if that's available. Could you confirm the total price including all taxes, please?"

Life Example

Booking hotel. Decision: Polite request ("I'd like to book a double room with a sea view for three nights, if that's available...")

Business Example

Booking conference room. Decision: Polite request ("I'd like to book the main conference room for 20 people on Friday, if it's available...")

⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Polite) Gets better service, builds relationship.
Lose (Polite) May feel too indirect in some situations.

Common Mistake

Being too direct ("I want to book a room") instead of polite ("I'd like to book a room, please").

Reflection: Am I being polite enough?

4

Confirmation (How do I confirm?)

"How do I confirm the booking?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐ High The Confirmation Decision

3-Level Examples

Simple (B2) "OK, I'll book it."
Natural (B2+) "Could you confirm the booking details, please?"
C1 Professional "Could you confirm the booking details, including the check-in and check-out times, the room type, and the total price? Could you send me a confirmation email, please?"

Life Example

Booking hotel. Decision: Confirm all details ("Could you confirm the booking details, including the check-in and check-out times...")

Business Example

Booking conference room. Decision: Confirm all details ("Could you confirm the booking details, including the date, time, and equipment...")

⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Confirm) Avoids confusion, gets exactly what you want.
Lose (Confirm) Takes more time to confirm all details.

Common Mistake

Assuming the booking is correct instead of confirming all details.

Reflection: Am I confirming all details?

5

Modification (How do I modify?)

"How do I modify the booking?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐ High The Modification Decision

3-Level Examples

Simple (B2) "I want to change my booking."
Natural (B2+) "I'd like to change my booking from Friday to Saturday, if that's possible."
C1 Professional "I'd like to modify my booking, if that's possible. I'd like to change the check-in date from Friday the 15th to Saturday the 16th, and extend the stay by one night. Could you confirm if that's possible and what the price difference would be?"

Life Example

Modifying hotel booking. Decision: Clear modification request ("I'd like to modify my booking, if that's possible. I'd like to change the check-in date...")

Business Example

Modifying conference room booking. Decision: Clear modification request ("I'd like to modify my booking, if that's possible. I'd like to change the date...")

⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Clear modification) Gets exactly what you want, avoids confusion.
Lose (Clear modification) May not be possible, may have extra charges.

Common Mistake

Being vague ("I want to change my booking") instead of clear ("I'd like to change the check-in date from Friday to Saturday...").

Reflection: Am I being clear about what I want to modify?

3 Common Mistakes

Traps that destroy service requests

Mistake 1: Being Vague

❌ "I want to book a room."
✅ "I'd like to book a double room with a sea view for three nights, checking in on Friday."
Why: Vague requests get confused results. Clear requests get exactly what you want.

Mistake 2: Being Too Direct

❌ "I want a room now."
✅ "I'd like to book a room, please, if that's available."
Why: Too direct gets poor service. Polite requests get better service.

Mistake 3: Not Confirming Details

❌ "OK, I'll book it."
✅ "Could you confirm the booking details, including the check-in time and total price?"
Why: Not confirming leads to confusion. Confirming all details avoids problems.

🏨 Case 1: Life - Booking a Hotel Room

Context: You're calling a hotel to book a room for a weekend getaway. You need to be clear, specific, and polite to get exactly what you want.

You: "Hello, I'd like to book a room for this weekend, if that's available. I'd like a double room with a sea view for two nights, checking in on Friday the 15th at 3pm and checking out on Sunday the 17th at 11am."

Hotel: "Yes, we have availability. We have a double room with a sea view available for those dates."

You: "Great. Could you confirm the total price including breakfast and all taxes? And could you let me know what time check-in is?"

Hotel: "The total price is $250 per night, including breakfast and all taxes. Check-in is at 3pm."

You: "Perfect. Could you confirm the booking details, including the check-in time, the room type, and the total price? And could you send me a confirmation email, please?"

Hotel: "Yes, I'll send you a confirmation email with all the details."

You: "Perfect. Could you confirm your email address, please? And could you let me know if there's a cancellation policy?"

Hotel: "Our email is bookings@hotel.com. We have a free cancellation policy up to 24 hours before check-in."

You: "Perfect. Could you confirm the booking, please? My name is John Smith, and my email is john.smith@email.com."

Hotel: "Perfect, Mr. Smith. Your booking is confirmed. You'll receive a confirmation email shortly."

You: "Perfect. Thank you very much for your help. Have a great day."

Micro Writing: Service Request Messages (with Model Answers)

️ 4 Writing Tasks with Model Answers

Task 1: Hotel Booking Email

Context: You're emailing a hotel to book a room for a business trip. Write a clear, specific, and polite email to book the room.

Requirements:
  • Use 3 collocations from this lesson
  • Use 2 polite structures (e.g., "I'd like to...", "Could you...")
  • Use 2 confirmation structures (e.g., "Could you confirm...")
  • Length: 100-150 words
Model Answer (C1)

"Dear Sir/Madam, I'd like to book a room for my business trip next week. I'd like a single room with a desk for five nights, checking in on Monday the 15th at 3pm and checking out on Saturday the 20th at 11am. Could you confirm the total price including breakfast and all taxes? Could you confirm the booking details, including the check-in time, the room type, and the total price? Could you send me a confirmation email, please? Thank you very much for your help. Kind regards, John Smith."

Language Breakdown
  • Polite structures: "I'd like to...", "Could you..."
  • Collocations: "business trip", "single room", "total price"
  • Confirmation structures: "Could you confirm...", "Could you send..."
  • Polite closing: "Thank you very much for your help. Kind regards..."
Alternative Version (Casual)

"Hi, I'd like to book a room for next week. I'd like a single room for five nights, from Monday to Saturday. Could you let me know the total price? Could you send me a confirmation email? Thanks, John."

Self-check Checklist
  • □ đủ word count (100-150 words)

  • □ dùng đủ 2 polite structures (I'd like to, Could you)

  • □ có opening → details → confirmation → closing

Integrated Dialogues (Long Format - 15-20 turns)

💬 Integrated Dialogues (15-20 turns with evolution)

🏨 Dialogue 1: Booking a Hotel Room (Life)

Context: You're calling a hotel to book a room for a weekend getaway. Conversation evolves through request → specify → confirm.
You:
"Hello, I'd like to book a room for this weekend, if that's available. I'd like a double room with a sea view for two nights, checking in on Friday the 15th at 3pm and checking out on Sunday the 17th at 11am." Request
Hotel:
"Yes, we have availability. We have a double room with a sea view available for those dates."
You:
"Great. Could you confirm the total price including breakfast and all taxes? And could you let me know what time check-in is?" Specify
Hotel:
"The total price is $250 per night, including breakfast and all taxes. Check-in is at 3pm."
You:
"Perfect. Could you confirm the booking details, including the check-in time, the room type, and the total price? And could you send me a confirmation email, please?" Confirm
Hotel:
"Yes, I'll send you a confirmation email with all the details."
You:
"Perfect. Could you confirm your email address, please? And could you let me know if there's a cancellation policy?" Confirm
Hotel:
"Our email is bookings@hotel.com. We have a free cancellation policy up to 24 hours before check-in."
You:
"Perfect. Could you confirm the booking, please? My name is John Smith, and my email is john.smith@email.com." Confirm
Hotel:
"Perfect, Mr. Smith. Your booking is confirmed. You'll receive a confirmation email shortly."
You:
"Perfect. Thank you very much for your help. Have a great day."

Decision Point Analysis

  • Decision: Combined Request (clear) + Specify (specific) + Confirm (all details).
  • Alternative Decision: Be vague. (Risk: Get confused results).
  • Why this decision? Clear request gets exactly what you want. Specific details avoid confusion. Confirming all details avoids problems.
  • Evolution: Request → Specify → Confirm → Confirm details → Confirm booking.

Strategic Grammar in Context (5 C1 Patterns)

📝 Strategic Grammar Toolkit (5 C1 Patterns with explanation, examples, common mistakes, native alternatives)

Pattern 1: Polite Requests with "I'd like to"

Used to make polite requests. These structures are more polite than "I want to" and get better service.
  • "I'd like to book a room."
  • "I'd like to book a table."
  • "I'd like to order room service."
  • "I'd like to modify my booking."
  • "I'd like to cancel my booking."
Common Mistake: "I want to book a room." (Too direct, less polite)
Native Alternative: "I'd like to book a room, if that's available."

Pattern 2: Polite Requests with "Could you"

Used to make polite requests. These structures are more polite than "Can you" and get better service.
  • "Could you confirm the booking?"
  • "Could you let me know the total price?"
  • "Could you send me a confirmation email?"
  • "Could you confirm the booking details?"
  • "Could you let me know if there's a cancellation policy?"
Common Mistake: "Can you confirm the booking?" (Less polite than "Could you")
Native Alternative: "Could you confirm the booking, please?"

Pattern 3: Specificity with "including"

Used to be specific about details. These structures avoid confusion and get exactly what you want.
  • "Could you confirm the total price including breakfast and all taxes?"
  • "Could you confirm the booking details, including the check-in time, the room type, and the total price?"
  • "Could you let me know if there's a dress code?"
  • "Could you let me know if you have any dietary restrictions we should know about?"
  • "Could you let me know if there's a cancellation policy?"
Common Mistake: "Could you confirm the price?" (Not specific enough)
Native Alternative: "Could you confirm the total price including all taxes and fees?"

Pattern 4: Polite Conditions with "if that's"

Used to make polite conditions. These structures are more polite and get better service.
  • "I'd like to book a room, if that's available."
  • "I'd like a table for four at 7pm, if that's possible."
  • "I'd like to modify my booking, if that's possible."
  • "I'd like to cancel my booking, if that's possible."
  • "I'd like a late check-out, if that's available."
Common Mistake: "I want to book a room." (No polite condition)
Native Alternative: "I'd like to book a room, if that's available, please."

Pattern 5: Polite Closing with "Thank you"

Used to close politely. These structures are more polite and get better service.
  • "Thank you very much for your help."
  • "Thank you for your help."
  • "Thank you very much."
  • "Thank you for your assistance."
  • "Thank you for your time."
Common Mistake: "Thanks." (Too casual for professional situations)
Native Alternative: "Thank you very much for your help. Have a great day."
