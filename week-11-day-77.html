5 Reality Task Lenses

Every reality task requires five decisions. These are your 5 lenses:

1

Identification (What issue have I been avoiding?)

"What real household issue have I been avoiding?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Identification Decision

Decision Cue

  • Urgent issue → Unresolved complaint with landlord
  • Ongoing issue → Boundary issue with roommate
  • Avoided issue → Unpaid invoice or unresolved repair

Life Example

Unresolved repair request. Decision: Identify the specific issue ("The leaking faucet I've been avoiding")

Business Example

Unpaid invoice. Decision: Identify the specific issue ("The invoice from 3 months ago I haven't addressed")

Register Progression

🏠 Casual "The issue I've been avoiding..."
☕ Neutral "The issue I haven't addressed..."
Professional "The unresolved issue that requires attention..."
📋 Formal "The outstanding matter that requires immediate attention..."
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Honest identification) Clarity, direction for action.
Lose (Honest identification) May feel uncomfortable to admit.

Common Mistake

Choosing a "safe" issue instead of the real issue you've been avoiding.

Reflection: Am I being honest about what I've been avoiding?

2

Framework (Which framework will I use?)

"Which framework from Week 11 will I use?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Framework Decision

Decision Cue

  • Written complaint → Complaint Email Loop (Day 75)
  • Unresolved issue → Escalation Loop (Day 76)
  • Personal boundary → Boundary-Setting Framework (Day 74)
  • Mutual solution → Negotiation Flow (Week 9)

Life Example

Leaking faucet. Decision: Complaint Email Loop (write to landlord)

Business Example

Unpaid invoice. Decision: Escalation Loop (escalate to manager)

Register Progression

🏠 Casual "I'll use the complaint framework..."
Neutral "I'll apply the escalation framework..."
💼 Professional "I'll apply the appropriate framework..."
📋 Formal "I'll apply the most appropriate framework..."
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Right framework) Structure, confidence in approach.
Lose (Wrong framework) May not fit the situation.

Common Mistake

Using the wrong framework for the situation (e.g., using escalation for a simple complaint).

Reflection: Am I choosing the right framework for this situation?

3

Language (What language will I use?)

"What language will I use? Assertive? Professional? Diplomatic?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Language Decision

Decision Cue

  • Assertive → "I need this resolved by Friday."
  • Professional → "I would appreciate your prompt attention to this matter."
  • Diplomatic → "I would appreciate it if this could be addressed at your earliest convenience."

Life Example

Leaking faucet. Decision: Assertive ("I need this resolved by Friday")

Business Example

Unpaid invoice. Decision: Professional ("I would appreciate your prompt attention to this matter")

Register Progression

🏠 Casual "I need this fixed by Friday."
☕ Neutral "I'd appreciate your help with this."
💼 Professional "I would appreciate your prompt attention to this matter."
📋 Formal "I would appreciate it if this could be addressed at your earliest convenience."
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Assertive) Clear expectations, gets results.
Lose (Assertive) May feel too demanding.

Common Mistake

Using language that's too aggressive or too passive for the situation.

Reflection: Am I using the right language for this situation?

4

Action (Will I take action today?)

"Will I actually send/deliver it today? Not tomorrow — today."

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Action Decision

Decision Cue

  • Email → Send it now (not save as draft)
  • Conversation → Schedule it for today or tomorrow
  • Letter → Write it and commit to delivering it

Life Example

Email to landlord. Decision: Send it now (not save as draft)

Business Example

Email to client. Decision: Send it now (not save as draft)

Gain (Action today) Momentum, results, growth.

Reflection (What did I learn?)

"What did I learn from this experience?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐ High The Reflection Decision

Decision Cue

  • What worked? → What frameworks/language worked well?
  • What didn't? → What was difficult or uncomfortable?
  • What would I change? → What would I do differently next time?

Life Example

After sending email. Decision: Reflect on what worked and what didn't

Business Example

After having conversation. Decision: Reflect on what worked and what didn't

📋 Formal "What lessons did I learn from this experience?"
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Reflection) Learning, growth, improvement.
Lose (Reflection) Takes time, may feel uncomfortable.

Common Mistake

Skipping reflection and moving on without learning from the experience.

Reflection: Will I take time to reflect and learn from this experience?

3 Common Mistakes

Traps that destroy reality tasks

Mistake 1: Choosing a "Safe" Issue

❌ Choosing an easy issue instead of the real issue you've been avoiding
✅ Choosing the real issue you've been avoiding, even if it's uncomfortable
Why: Safe issues don't create growth. Real issues create growth, even when uncomfortable.

Mistake 2: Saving as Draft "to Send Later"

❌ Writing the email but saving it as draft "to send later"
✅ Sending it today, even if it's not perfect
Why: "Later" often means "never." Action today creates momentum.

Mistake 3: Skipping Reflection

❌ Moving on without reflecting on what you learned
✅ Taking time to reflect on what worked and what didn't
Why: Reflection turns experience into learning. Without reflection, you don't grow.

🏠 Case 1: Life - Unresolved Complaint with Landlord

Context: You've had a leaking faucet for 2 weeks. You've been avoiding sending a complaint email to your landlord. Today, you'll apply the Complaint Email Loop from Day 75.

Your Decision:

  • Lens 1 (Identification): The leaking faucet I've been avoiding for 2 weeks

  • Lens 3 (Language): Assertive but professional

  • Lens 5 (Reflection): Reflect on what worked and what didn't

BREAKDOWN (Deep Analysis)

"I am writing regarding the leaking faucet in the bathroom that has been ongoing for 2 weeks."
🎯 Communication Goal: Open with clarity and documentation. 🧠 Hidden Message: "This is a real issue that needs attention." ⚖️ Trade-off: Direct but professional. 🔄 Better Alternative? "There's a leak." (Too vague, doesn't show urgency). ⭐ Why This Wins: Clear, documented, professional.
"I would appreciate your prompt attention to this matter, as it's affecting my daily use of the bathroom."
🎯 Communication Goal: State impact and request action. 🧠 Hidden Message: "This is affecting me and needs resolution." ⚖️ Trade-off: Assertive but professional. 🔄 Better Alternative? "Please fix it." (Too brief, doesn't show impact). ⭐ Why This Wins: Shows impact, requests action professionally.
"I would appreciate it if this could be resolved by Friday."
🎯 Communication Goal: Set clear deadline. Hidden Message: "I expect this to be resolved by a specific time." ️ Trade-off: Clear expectations, may feel demanding. 🔄 Better Alternative? "Please fix it soon." (Vague, no deadline). ⭐ Why This Wins: Clear deadline creates accountability.

Strategic Grammar in Context

📝 Strategic Grammar Toolkit (5 C1 Patterns)

Pattern 1: Professional Opening

Used to open with clarity and documentation
  • Variant A: "I am writing regarding..."
  • Variant B: "I am writing to follow up on..."
  • Variant C: "I am writing to address..."
Examples:
  • "I am writing regarding the leaking faucet."
  • "I am writing to follow up on invoice #12345."
  • "I am writing to address the ongoing issue."
  • "I am writing regarding the matter that requires attention."
  • "I am writing to follow up on the outstanding matter."

Pattern 2: Stating Impact

Used to state impact and request action
  • Variant A: "I would appreciate your prompt attention to this matter."
  • Variant B: "I would appreciate your immediate attention to this matter."
  • Variant C: "I would appreciate it if this could be addressed."
Examples:
  • "I would appreciate your prompt attention to this matter."
  • "I would appreciate your immediate attention to this matter."
  • "I would appreciate it if this could be addressed."
  • "I would appreciate your attention to this urgent matter."
  • "I would appreciate it if this could be resolved."

Pattern 3: Setting Deadline

Used to set clear deadline
  • Variant A: "I would appreciate it if this could be resolved by [date]."
  • Variant B: "I would appreciate it if this could be addressed by [timeframe]."
  • Variant C: "I would appreciate your response by [date]."
Examples:
  • "I would appreciate it if this could be resolved by Friday."
  • "I would appreciate it if this could be addressed by the end of this week."
  • "I would appreciate your response by Friday."
  • "I would appreciate it if this could be resolved by [specific date]."
  • "I would appreciate your prompt response to this matter."

Pattern 4: Taking Action

Used to take action today
  • Variant A: "I will send it today."
  • Variant B: "I will take action today."
  • Variant C: "I will take immediate action on this matter."
Examples:
  • "I will send it today."
  • "I will take action today."
  • "I will take immediate action on this matter."
  • "I will send the email today."
  • "I will take action on this matter today."

Pattern 5: Reflecting

Used to reflect on the experience
  • Variant A: "What did I learn from this experience?"
  • Variant B: "What worked and what didn't?"
  • Variant C: "What would I do differently next time?"
Examples:
  • "What did I learn from this experience?"
  • "What worked and what didn't?"
  • "What would I do differently next time?"
  • "What lessons did I learn from this experience?"
  • "What did I learn about my default style?"
