Today's Philosophy

Recommending = Understanding Preferences + Vivid Description + Tailored Recommendation

"Recommending a restaurant or dish isn't about saying 'It's good.' It's about understanding preferences, painting a vivid picture with sensory details, and tailoring the recommendation to the listener."

Every recommendation requires you to make five decisions simultaneously:

Preference Decision: How do I understand preferences? (Generic? Tailored?)
Description Decision: How do I describe? (Basic? Vivid?)
Sensory Decision: How do I use sensory details? (None? Sensory?)
Tailoring Decision: How do I tailor? (Generic? Tailored?)
Enthusiasm Decision: How do I show enthusiasm? (Flat? Enthusiastic?)

Important: Day 112 teaches you how to recommend restaurants and dishes with vivid description, tailoring, and enthusiasm.

The Recommendation Loop

The 4-step process for recommending a restaurant or dish

1
ASK PREFERENCES (Understand preferences)
How do I ask? "What kind of food are you in the mood for?" / "Do you have any dietary preferences?" (Understand preferences)
2
DESCRIBE VIVIDLY (Paint a vivid picture)
How do I describe? "It has this wonderful [flavor]..." / "The atmosphere is absolutely incredible..." (Describe vividly)
3
MATCH PREFERENCES (Tailor to preferences)
How do I match? "Since you love [flavor], you'll absolutely love..." / "Given your preference for..., you'll love..." (Tailor to preferences)
4
RECOMMEND ENTHUSIASTICALLY (Make enthusiastic recommendation)
How do I recommend? "You absolutely must try..." / "You can't go wrong with..." (Recommend enthusiastically)

5 Recommendation Lenses (3-Level Examples)

Every recommendation requires five decisions. Each lens has 3 levels of examples: Simple → Natural → C1 Professional.

1

Understanding Preferences

"How do I understand preferences?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Preference Decision

3-Level Examples

Simple (B2) "Do you like Italian food?"
Natural (B2+) "What kind of food are you in the mood for?"
C1 Professional "What kind of cuisine are you in the mood for tonight? Do you have any dietary preferences or restrictions I should know about?"

Life Example

Recommending to a friend. Decision: Understanding preferences ("What kind of cuisine are you in the mood for tonight?")

Business Example

Recommending to a client. Decision: Professional understanding ("Do you have any dietary preferences or restrictions?")

⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Understanding) Tailors recommendation, shows you care about their preferences.
Lose (Understanding) Takes more time to ask, but ensures better recommendation.

Common Mistake

Generic recommendations without asking preferences. Native speakers ask about preferences to tailor recommendations.

Reflection: Am I understanding preferences or giving generic recommendations?

3 Common Mistakes

Traps that destroy recommendations

Mistake 1: Generic Recommendations

❌ "It's good, you should try it."
✅ "You absolutely must try the [dish] — it has this wonderful [flavor] and the atmosphere is absolutely incredible."
Why: Generic recommendations don't paint a picture. Vivid descriptions make them want to try it.

Mistake 2: Not Asking About Preferences

❌ "You should try this restaurant."
✅ "What kind of cuisine are you in the mood for? Do you have any dietary preferences?"
Why: Not asking about preferences leads to irrelevant recommendations. Asking ensures relevant recommendations.

Mistake 3: Not Using Sensory Details

❌ "It tastes good."
✅ "The aroma is absolutely incredible — it's this wonderful blend of spices. The texture is incredibly tender."
Why: No sensory details doesn't create a vivid picture. Sensory details create vivid mental images.

Micro Writing: Recommendation Messages (with Model Answers)

✍️ 4 Writing Tasks with Model Answers

Task 1: Recommending a Restaurant to a Friend

Context: Your friend is visiting your city. Write a message recommending your favorite restaurant.

Requirements:
  • Use 3 collocations from this lesson
  • Use 2 vivid description structures (e.g., "The [food] is this wonderful...", "The atmosphere is...")
  • Use 1 enthusiasm structure (e.g., "You absolutely must try...")
  • Length: 100-150 words
Model Answer (C1)

"You absolutely must try this Italian place I know. Since you love Italian food, you'll absolutely love this place. The pasta is handmade daily, and the sauce is this wonderful rich, creamy tomato sauce. The atmosphere is absolutely incredible — it's this wonderful cozy, authentic Italian atmosphere. It's incredibly reasonable for the quality. It's hands down the best Italian restaurant in the city. You can't go wrong with this place. You're going to absolutely love it."

Language Breakdown
  • Vivid Description: "The pasta is handmade daily", "The sauce is this wonderful rich, creamy tomato sauce"
  • Enthusiasm: "You absolutely must try this", "You're going to absolutely love it"
  • Collocations: "hands down the best", "can't go wrong", "absolutely incredible"
Alternative Version (Casual)

"You absolutely must try this Italian place. Since you love Italian, you'll love it. The pasta is handmade, and the sauce is incredible. The atmosphere is amazing. It's the best Italian place in the city. You're going to love it."

Self-check Checklist
  • □ đủ word count (100-150 words)
  • □ dùng đủ 3 collocations
  • □ dùng đủ 2 vivid description structures
  • □ dùng đủ 1 enthusiasm structure
  • □ có opening → description → enthusiasm → closing

Integrated Dialogues (Long Format - 15-20 turns)

💬 Integrated Dialogues (15-20 turns with evolution)

🏠 Dialogue 1: Recommending a Local Favorite to a Visiting Friend (Life)

Context: Your friend is visiting your city. You want to recommend your favorite local restaurant and dish. Conversation evolves through asking preferences → describing vividly → matching preferences → recommending enthusiastically.
Friend:
"I'm so excited to be here! Where should we eat tonight?"
You:
"What kind of cuisine are you in the mood for tonight? Do you have any dietary preferences or restrictions I should know about?" Ask Preferences
Friend:
"I love Italian food, and I'm not too spicy."
You:
"Perfect! Since you love Italian food, you'll absolutely love this place. The pasta is handmade daily, and the sauce is this wonderful rich, creamy tomato sauce. The atmosphere is absolutely incredible — it's this wonderful cozy, authentic Italian atmosphere." Describe Vividly + Match Preferences
Friend:
"That sounds amazing! Is it expensive?"
You:
"It's incredibly reasonable for the quality. It's hands down the best Italian restaurant in the city. You can't go wrong with this place." Recommend Enthusiastically
Friend:
"I'm sold! Let's go there tonight."
You:
"You're going to absolutely love it. It's the kind of place that you'll want to come back to again and again." Enthusiasm

Decision Point Analysis

  • Decision: Combined Ask Preferences (understand preferences) + Describe Vividly (paint vivid picture) + Match Preferences (tailor to preferences) + Recommend Enthusiastically (make enthusiastic recommendation).
  • Alternative Decision: Generic recommendation. (Risk: Irrelevant recommendation, doesn't excite them).
  • Why this decision? Asking preferences ensures relevant recommendation. Describing vividly paints a picture. Matching preferences shows you listen. Recommending enthusiastically makes them want to try it.
  • Resistance handled: Friend's dietary preferences. You asked about preferences, described vividly, matched preferences, and recommended enthusiastically.
  • Evolution: Ask preferences → Describe vividly → Match preferences → Recommend enthusiastically → Friend excited.

Strategic Grammar in Context (5 C1 Patterns)

📝 Strategic Grammar Toolkit (5 C1 Patterns)

Pattern 1: Asking Preferences with "What kind of cuisine are you in the mood for"

Used to ask about preferences. These structures show you care about their preferences.
  • "What kind of cuisine are you in the mood for?"
  • "What kind of food are you in the mood for?"
  • "Do you have any dietary preferences?"
  • "Do you have any dietary restrictions I should know about?"
  • "What kind of cuisine do you prefer?"
Common Mistake: "Do you like Italian food?" (Too limited)
Native Alternative: "What kind of cuisine are you in the mood for?"

Pattern 2: Describing Vividly with "The [food] is this wonderful..."

Used to describe vividly. These structures paint a vivid picture.
  • "The pasta is this wonderful rich, creamy tomato sauce."
  • "The atmosphere is absolutely incredible."
  • "The food is exceptional."
  • "The steak is this wonderful tender, flavorful steak."
  • "It's incredibly cozy."
Common Mistake: "It's good." (Too basic)
Native Alternative: "The [food] is this wonderful [description]."

Pattern 3: Matching Preferences with "Since you love..."

Used to match preferences. These structures show you listen and tailor recommendations.
  • "Since you love Italian food, you'll absolutely love this place."
  • "Given your preference for upscale dining, you'll absolutely love this place."
  • "Since you prefer milder flavors, you'll love this dish."
  • "Given that you need a quiet place, you'll love this cafe."
  • "Since you love [flavor], you'll love this dish."
Common Mistake: "You should try this restaurant." (Generic)
Native Alternative: "Since you love [flavor], you'll absolutely love this place."

Pattern 4: Recommending Enthusiastically with "You absolutely must try"

Used to recommend enthusiastically. These structures show genuine enthusiasm.
  • "You absolutely must try this."
  • "You absolutely must try the [dish]."
  • "You can't go wrong with this place."
  • "You're going to absolutely love it."
  • "It's hands down the best."
Common Mistake: "It's good, you should try it." (Flat)
Native Alternative: "You absolutely must try this."

Pattern 5: Describing Quality with "It's incredibly..."

Used to describe quality vividly. These structures describe quality vividly.
  • "It's incredibly reasonable for the quality."
  • "It's incredibly cozy."
  • "The service is exceptional."
  • "The food is exceptional."
  • "It's perfect for business meetings."
Common Mistake: "It's good." (Too basic)
Native Alternative: "It's incredibly [adjective]."
