Today's Philosophy

Discussing Ethics = Expressing Respectfully + Acknowledging Views + Navigating Disagreements + Finding Common Ground

"Discussing ethics and morality isn't about judging others. It's about expressing your views respectfully, acknowledging different perspectives, navigating disagreements with respect, and finding common ground."

Every ethics discussion requires you to make five decisions simultaneously:

Respectful Decision: How do I express respectfully? (Judgmental? Respectful?)
Acknowledgment Decision: How do I acknowledge views? (Dismiss? Acknowledge?)
Disagreement Decision: How do I navigate disagreements? (Argumentative? Respectful?)
Common Ground Decision: How do I find common ground? (None? Some?)
Understanding Decision: How do I seek understanding? (Judge? Understand?)

Important: Day 135 teaches you how to discuss ethics and morality with respect, acknowledgment, and understanding.

The Ethics Discussion Loop

The 4-step process for discussing ethics and morality respectfully

1
EXPRESS RESPECTFULLY (Express your views respectfully)
How do I express? "I see it differently..." / "I see it from a different perspective..." (Express respectfully)
2
ACKNOWLEDGE VIEWS (Acknowledge different perspectives)
How do I acknowledge? "I understand your perspective..." / "I can see where you're coming from..." (Acknowledge respectfully)
3
NAVIGATE DISAGREEMENTS (Navigate disagreements respectfully)
How do I navigate? "I'd like to understand your perspective better..." / "I'd like to understand where you're coming from..." (Navigate respectfully)
4
FIND COMMON GROUND (Find common ground)
How do I find common ground? "I think we can agree that..." / "I think we both agree that..." (Find common ground)

3 Common Mistakes

Traps that destroy ethics discussions

Mistake 1: Judging Instead of Discussing Respectfully

❌ "That's wrong! That's immoral!"
✅ "I see it differently, and I'd like to understand your perspective better. I see it from a different perspective."
Why: Judging prevents open discussion. Discussing respectfully keeps the conversation open.

Mistake 2: Dismissing Views Instead of Acknowledging

❌ "I don't agree with that."
✅ "I understand your perspective, and I can see where you're coming from. I'd like to understand your perspective better."
Why: Dismissing views prevents open discussion. Acknowledging views keeps the conversation open.

Mistake 3: Being Argumentative Instead of Navigating Respectfully

❌ "You're wrong! You don't understand!"
✅ "I'd like to understand your perspective better. I'd like to understand where you're coming from."
Why: Being argumentative prevents open discussion. Navigating respectfully keeps the conversation open.

Micro Writing: Ethics Discussion Messages (with Model Answers)

️ 4 Writing Tasks with Model Answers

Task 1: Ethical Dilemma at Work

Context: You're discussing an ethical dilemma at work with a colleague. Write a message expressing your views respectfully.

Requirements:
  • Use 3 collocations from this lesson
  • Use 2 respectful expression structures (e.g., "I see it differently...", "I see it from a different perspective...")
  • Use 2 understanding structures (e.g., "I'd like to understand your perspective...", "I'd like to understand where you're coming from...")
  • Length: 100-150 words
Model Answer (C1)

"I understand your perspective on this ethical dilemma. I see it differently, and I'd like to understand your perspective better. I see it from a different perspective. I appreciate your understanding. I'd like to understand your perspective better. I'd like to understand where you're coming from. I see it from a different perspective. I appreciate your understanding. I think we can agree that... I think we both agree that... I think we can find some common ground."

Language Breakdown
  • Respectful Expression: "I see it differently...", "I see it from a different perspective..."
  • Collocations: "understand your perspective", "understand where you're coming from", "find some common ground"
  • Understanding: "I'd like to understand your perspective...", "I'd like to understand where you're coming from..."
  • Cohesion markers: "I understand", "I see it differently", "I think we can agree"
Alternative Version (Professional)

"I understand your perspective on this ethical dilemma. I see it differently, and I'd like to understand your perspective better. I see it from a different perspective. I appreciate your understanding. I'd like to understand your perspective better. I'd like to understand where you're coming from. I see it from a different perspective. I appreciate your understanding. I think we can agree that... I think we both agree that... I think we can find some common ground."
