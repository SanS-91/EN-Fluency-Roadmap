5 Health Check-in Lenses

Checking in on someone's health requires five decisions. These are your 5 lenses:

1

Empathy (How do I show empathy?)

"How do I show empathy when checking in?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Empathy Decision

Decision Cue

  • Empathetic → "I can see you're not feeling well." (Empathetic, caring)
  • Not empathetic → "You look sick." (Not empathetic, not caring)
  • Very empathetic → "I can see you're going through a tough time." (Very empathetic, very caring)

Life Example

Friend is sick. Decision: Empathetic ("I can see you're not feeling well.")

Business Example

Colleague is sick. Decision: Empathetic ("I can see you're not feeling well.")

Register Progression

🏠 Casual "I can see you're not feeling well."
☕ Neutral "I can see you're going through a tough time."
💼 Professional "I can see you're not feeling well. I'm here for you."
📋 Formal "I can see you're going through a difficult time. I'm here to support you."
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Empathetic) Shows care, builds connection.
Lose (Empathetic) May feel too personal in professional settings.

Common Mistake

Not being empathetic ("You look sick") instead of showing empathy.

Reflection: Am I showing empathy or not?

2

Care (How do I show care?)

"How do I show care when checking in?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Care Decision

Decision Cue

  • Caring → "I'm here for you." (Caring, supportive)
  • Not caring → "That's your problem." (Not caring, not supportive)
  • Very caring → "I'm here for you. Is there anything I can do?" (Very caring, very supportive)

Life Example

Friend is sick. Decision: Caring ("I'm here for you.")

Business Example

Colleague is sick. Decision: Caring ("I'm here for you.")

Register Progression

🏠 Casual "I'm here for you."
☕ Neutral "I'm here to support you."
💼 Professional "I'm here to support you. Is there anything I can do?"
📋 Formal "I'm here to support you. Please let me know if there's anything I can do."
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Caring) Shows support, builds connection.
Lose (Caring) May feel too personal in professional settings.

Common Mistake

Not showing care ("That's your problem") instead of showing care.

Reflection: Am I showing care or not?

3

Respect (How do I show respect?)

"How do I show respect when checking in?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Respect Decision

Decision Cue

  • Respectful → "I respect your boundaries." (Respectful, respectful)
  • Not respectful → "You should tell me." (Not respectful, disrespectful)
  • Very respectful → "I respect your right to privacy." (Very respectful, very respectful)

Life Example

Friend doesn't want to talk. Decision: Respectful ("I respect your boundaries.")

Business Example

Colleague doesn't want to talk. Decision: Respectful ("I respect your boundaries.")

Register Progression

🏠 Casual "I respect your boundaries."
☕ Neutral "I respect your privacy."

📋 Formal "I respect your right to privacy on this matter."
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Respectful) Shows respect, keeps relationship strong.
Lose (Respectful) May feel like you're not being helpful.

Not respecting boundaries ("You should tell me") instead of respecting boundaries.

4

Question (How do I ask the right questions?)

"How do I ask the right questions when checking in?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Question Decision

Decision Cue

  • Right questions → "How are you feeling?" (Right questions, caring)
  • Wrong questions → "What's wrong with you?" (Wrong questions, not caring)
  • Very right questions → "How are you feeling today? Is there anything I can do?" (Very right questions, very caring)

3 Common Mistakes

Traps that destroy health check-ins

Mistake 1: Not Showing Empathy

❌ "You look sick."
✅ "I can see you're not feeling well."
Why: Not showing empathy creates distance. Showing empathy builds connection.

Mistake 2: Not Showing Care

"That's your problem."
✅ "I'm here for you."
Why: Not showing care creates distance. Showing care builds connection.

Mistake 3: Not Respecting Boundaries

❌ "You should tell me."
✅ "I respect your boundaries."
Why: Not respecting boundaries creates conflict. Respecting boundaries keeps relationship strong.

🎯 BREAKDOWN (Deep Analysis)

"I can see you're not feeling well."
🎯 Communication Goal: Show empathy. Hidden Message: "I care about you and I can see you're not feeling well." ⚖️ Trade-off: Takes more time, but shows empathy. 🔄 Better Alternative? "You look sick." (Not empathetic, not caring). ⭐ Why This Wins: Empathy shows care.
"I'm here for you."
🎯 Communication Goal: Show care. Hidden Message: "I'm here to support you." ⚖️ Trade-off: Takes more time, but shows care. 🔄 Better Alternative? "That's your problem." (Not caring, not supportive). ⭐ Why This Wins: Care shows support.
"Is there anything I can do?"
🎯 Communication Goal: Take action. 🧠 Hidden Message: "I'm here to help." ️ Trade-off: Takes more time, but shows support. 🔄 Better Alternative? "That's your problem." (Not supportive, not helpful). ⭐ Why This Wins: Action shows support.

Flashcard Review

Situation: You need to check in on someone's health

Wrong Instinct: "You look sick."

C1 Language: Empathy → Care → Respect → Question → Action.

Trade-off: Takes more time upfront, but shows empathy, care, and respect.

💡 Tip: In health check-ins, empathy shows care. Care shows support. Respect keeps relationship strong. Question gets information. Action shows support.
