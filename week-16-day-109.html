Hosting Guests and Making Them Feel Welcome

Day 105-108 taught you to describe food, explain culture, make recommendations, and make passionate recommendations. Day 109 teaches you how to host guests and make them feel welcome — welcoming them warmly, anticipating their needs, and making them feel completely at home. B2 speakers just say "Welcome." C1 speakers create a warm atmosphere and make guests feel truly cared for.

💡 C1 Insight
C1 Insight
B2 Speaker: Just says "Welcome" or "Make yourself at home." Doesn't anticipate needs or create a warm atmosphere.

C1 Speaker: Welcomes guests warmly, anticipates their needs, and makes them feel completely at home. Creates a warm atmosphere and makes guests feel truly cared for.

"B2 speakers welcome guests. C1 speakers make guests feel at home. The difference isn't vocabulary — it's anticipation and warmth."
"B2 speakers say 'Welcome, make yourself at home.' C1 speakers say 'Welcome! It's so wonderful to have you. Please make yourself completely at home. Can I get you anything?' The difference isn't vocabulary — it's anticipation and warmth."

Today's Philosophy

Hosting = Warm Welcome + Anticipating Needs + Making Them Feel at Home

"Hosting guests isn't just about having them over. It's about welcoming them warmly, anticipating their needs, and making them feel completely at home while creating a warm atmosphere."

Every hosting situation requires you to make five decisions simultaneously:

Welcome Decision: How do I welcome them? (Generic? Warm?)
Settle In Decision: How do I help them settle in? (Basic? Comfortable?)
Anticipate Decision: How do I anticipate needs? (Reactive? Proactive?)
Atmosphere Decision: How do I create atmosphere? (Basic? Warm?)
Farewell Decision: How do I say goodbye? (Abrupt? Warm?)

Important: Day 109 teaches you how to host guests with warmth, anticipation, and care.

The Hosting Loop

The 4-step process for hosting guests and making them feel welcome

1
WARM WELCOME (Welcome them warmly)
How do I welcome them? "Welcome! It's so wonderful to have you..." / "Please come in and make yourself completely at home..." (Welcome warmly)
2
SETTLE IN (Help them settle in)
How do I help them settle in? "Please make yourself completely comfortable..." / "Let me take your coat..." (Help them settle in)
3
ANTICIPATE NEEDS (Anticipate their needs)
How do I anticipate needs? "Can I get you anything to drink or eat?" / "Please let me know if you need absolutely anything..." (Anticipate proactively)
4
WARM FAREWELL (Say goodbye warmly)
How do I say goodbye? "It was so lovely having you..." / "Thank you so much for coming. Safe travels..." (Say goodbye warmly)

1

Warm Welcome

"How do I welcome them warmly?"

3-Level Examples

Common Mistake

Reflection: Am I giving warm farewell or abrupt?

3 Common Mistakes

Traps that destroy hosting

Mistake 1: Generic Welcome

❌ "Welcome."
✅ "Welcome! It's so wonderful to have you. Please come in and make yourself completely at home."
Why: Generic welcome doesn't make guests feel welcome. Warm welcome makes guests feel truly welcome.

Mistake 2: Ignoring Needs

❌ Not asking if they need anything.
✅ "Can I get you anything to drink or eat? Please let me know if you need absolutely anything."
Why: Ignoring needs makes guests feel uncared for. Anticipating needs makes guests feel cared for.

Mistake 3: Abrupt Farewell

"Bye."
✅ "It was so lovely having you. Thank you so much for coming. Safe travels!"
Why: Abrupt farewell leaves bad impression. Warm farewell leaves lasting impression.

Micro Writing: Hosting Messages (with Model Answers)

✍️ 4 Writing Tasks with Model Answers

Task 1: Welcome Message

Context: Your friend is visiting your home. Write a welcome message.

Requirements:
  • Use 3 collocations from this lesson
  • Use 2 welcoming structures (e.g., "It's so wonderful to have you...", "Please make yourself...")
  • Use 1 anticipating structure (e.g., "Can I get you anything...")
  • Length: 100-150 words
Model Answer (C1)

"Welcome! It's so wonderful to have you. Please come in and make yourself completely at home. Let me take your coat. Please make yourself completely comfortable. Can I get you anything to drink or eat? Please let me know if you need absolutely anything at all. This is your home away from home."

Language Breakdown
  • Welcoming: "It's so wonderful to have you", "Please make yourself completely at home"
  • Settling in: "Let me take your coat", "Please make yourself completely comfortable"
  • Anticipating: "Can I get you anything to drink or eat?"
Alternative Version (Casual)

"Welcome! It's so good to see you. Please make yourself at home. Can I get you anything? Please let me know if you need anything. This is your home."

Context: Your friend is visiting your home. Conversation evolves through warm welcome → settling in → anticipating needs → warm farewell.

Decision Point Analysis

  • Resistance handled: Friend visiting home. You welcomed them warmly, helped them settle in, anticipated needs, created atmosphere, and said goodbye warmly.
  • Evolution: Warm welcome → Settle in → Anticipate needs → Create atmosphere → Warm farewell.

Strategic Grammar in Context (5 C1 Patterns)

📝 Strategic Grammar Toolkit (5 C1 Patterns)

Pattern 1: Warm Welcome with "It's so wonderful to have you"

Used to give warm welcome. These structures make guests feel welcome.
  • "It's so wonderful to have you."
  • "It's so wonderful to have you here."
  • "It's so good to see you."
  • "It's so wonderful to have you all here."
  • "It's so wonderful to have you here with us."
Common Mistake: "Welcome." (Too generic)
Native Alternative: "It's so wonderful to have you."

Pattern 2: Settling In with "Please make yourself completely comfortable"

Used to help guests settle in. These structures make guests feel comfortable.
  • "Please make yourself completely comfortable."
  • "Please make yourself comfortable."
  • "Please make yourself at home."
  • "Please make yourself completely at home."
  • "Please make yourself at home. This is your home."
Common Mistake: "Sit down." (Too basic)
Native Alternative: "Please make yourself completely comfortable."

Pattern 3: Anticipating Needs with "Can I get you anything"

Used to anticipate needs. These structures show you care.
  • "Can I get you anything to drink or eat?"
  • "Can I get you anything?"
  • "Can I get you anything to drink?"
  • "Please let me know if you need anything."
  • "Please let me know if you need absolutely anything."
Common Mistake: "Do you want water?" (Too basic)
Native Alternative: "Can I get you anything to drink or eat?"

Pattern 4: Creating Atmosphere with "This is your home away from home"

Used to create atmosphere. These structures make guests feel at home.
  • "This is your home away from home."
  • "This is your home."
  • "Please make yourself at home. This is your home."
  • "Please make yourself completely at home."
  • "Please feel free to make yourself at home."
Common Mistake: "It's nice here." (Too basic)
Native Alternative: "This is your home away from home."

Pattern 5: Warm Farewell with "It was so lovely having you"

Used to give warm farewell. These structures leave lasting impression.
  • "It was so lovely having you."
  • "It was lovely having you."
  • "Thank you so much for coming."
  • "Thank you for coming."
  • "Safe travels and let's do this again soon!"
Common Mistake: "Bye." (Too abrupt)
Native Alternative: "It was so lovely having you."
