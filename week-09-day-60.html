The Salary Conversation Flow

The 4-step conversation sequence for navigating salary discussions with confidence

1
OPEN (Bring up salary diplomatically)
"I'd like to discuss my compensation..." / "Would it be possible to talk about..."
2
SUPPORT (Present value with evidence)
"Based on the impact I've delivered..." / "Over the past year, I've..."
3
NEGOTIATE (Handle resistance, make counter-offers)
"I understand the constraints. What if we explored..." / "Would it be possible to..."
4
CLOSE (End professionally)
"Thank you for considering this. When can we revisit?" / "I appreciate your time."

5 Salary Language Lenses

Every salary conversation requires five language decisions. These are your 5 lenses:

1

Opening (Direct vs. Diplomatic)

"How do I bring up salary politely?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Opening Decision

Decision Cue

  • High trust + clear context → Direct ("I'd like to discuss my compensation")
  • Medium trust + sensitive → Diplomatic ("Would it be possible to talk about...")
  • Low trust + formal → Very diplomatic ("I was wondering if we could discuss...")

Life Example

Partner asks about salary. Decision: Direct ("I make around X") because high trust. Bố mẹ hỏi lương. Decision: Diplomatic ("I'm comfortable sharing that I'm in the X range") because medium trust.

Business Example

Performance review. Decision: Direct ("I'd like to discuss my compensation") because clear context. Casual conversation with boss. Decision: Diplomatic ("Would it be possible to talk about my salary?") because sensitive.

Register Progression

🏠 Casual "I make around X" / "I'm in the X range"
☕ Neutral "I'd like to discuss my compensation"
💼 Professional "Would it be possible to talk about my salary?"
📋 Formal "I was wondering if we could discuss my compensation package"
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Diplomatic) Reduces tension, shows respect.
Lose (Diplomatic) May feel indirect.

Common Mistake

Being too direct ("I want more money") or too indirect ("Um, maybe we could...")

Reflection: Am I opening with confidence and diplomacy, or with apology or aggression?

2

Value Language (Facts vs. Claims)

"How do I present my contributions without sounding boastful?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Value Decision

Decision Cue

  • Strong evidence → Facts ("I delivered X, resulting in Y")
  • Moderate evidence → Balanced ("I've contributed to X, which has helped Y")
  • Weak evidence → Humble ("I've been working on X, and I'm proud of Y")

Life Example

Partner asks why you deserve a raise. Decision: Facts ("I've been working overtime and got a promotion"). Bố mẹ hỏi tại sao lương cao. Decision: Balanced ("I've been taking on more responsibilities").

Business Example

Performance review. Decision: Facts ("I led the X project, generating $500K in revenue"). Casual conversation. Decision: Balanced ("I've been contributing to several key projects").

Register Progression

🏠 Casual "I've been working hard and got good results"
☕ Neutral "I've delivered X, which has helped Y"
💼 Professional "Based on the impact I've delivered..."
📋 Formal "In light of my contributions to X, resulting in Y..."
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Facts) Harder to dismiss, shows evidence.
Lose (Facts) May sound boastful if not framed carefully.

Common Mistake

Making claims without evidence ("I deserve more") or being too humble ("I don't know if I deserve this")

Reflection: Am I presenting facts with confidence, or making claims without evidence?

3

Resistance Handling (Reactive vs. Composed)

"How do I respond to pushback without getting defensive?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Resistance Decision

Decision Cue

  • Soft pushback → Acknowledge ("I understand the constraints")
  • Hard pushback → Compose ("I appreciate your perspective. What if we explored...")
  • Emotional pushback → Stay calm ("I hear you. Let's look at this together")

Life Example

Partner says "We can't afford that." Decision: Acknowledge ("I understand. What if we looked at..."). Bố mẹ nói "Lương cao thế là tốt rồi." Decision: Compose ("I appreciate your perspective. For me, it's about...").

Business Example

Boss says "Budget is tight." Decision: Acknowledge ("I understand. What if we explored alternatives?"). Boss says "That's not possible." Decision: Compose ("I appreciate your perspective. What would make it possible?").

Register Progression

🏠 Casual "I get it. What if we tried..."
☕ Neutral "I understand. What if we explored..."
💼 Professional "I appreciate your perspective. What if we considered..."
📋 Formal "I acknowledge the constraints. Would it be possible to explore..."
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Composed) Keeps conversation productive.
Lose (Composed) May feel like you're not advocating for yourself.

Common Mistake

Getting defensive ("But I deserve this!") or giving up too easily ("Okay, never mind")

Reflection: Am I responding with composure and curiosity, or with defensiveness or defeat?

4

Negotiation Language (Demanding vs. Collaborative)

"How do I make counter-offers politely?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐⭐ Very High The Negotiation Decision

Decision Cue

  • Clear ask → Collaborative ("Would it be possible to...")
  • Flexible ask → Exploratory ("What if we considered...")
  • Firm ask → Diplomatic ("I'd like to propose...")

Life Example

Partner asks how much you need. Decision: Collaborative ("Would it be possible to adjust..."). Bố mẹ hỏi muốn gì. Decision: Exploratory ("What if we looked at...").

Business Example

Boss offers 10% raise. Decision: Collaborative ("Would it be possible to consider 15%?"). Boss says no raise. Decision: Exploratory ("What if we explored alternatives like bonus or equity?").

Register Progression

🏠 Casual "What if we tried..." / "Could we..."
☕ Neutral "Would it be possible to..."
💼 Professional "I'd like to propose..." / "What if we considered..."
📋 Formal "I would like to propose an adjustment to..."
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Collaborative) Shows flexibility, invites dialogue.
Lose (Collaborative) May feel like you're compromising.

Common Mistake

Being too demanding ("I need 20%") or too passive ("Whatever you think is fine")

Reflection: Am I negotiating with collaboration and diplomacy, or with demands or passivity?

5

Closing Language (Abrupt vs. Graceful)

"How do I end the conversation professionally?"

⭐⭐⭐⭐ High The Closing Decision

Decision Cue

  • Agreement reached → Grateful ("Thank you for considering this")
  • No agreement → Professional ("When can we revisit?")
  • Mixed outcome → Balanced ("I appreciate your time. Let's follow up on...")

Life Example

Partner agrees. Decision: Grateful ("Thanks for understanding"). Partner needs time. Decision: Professional ("When can we talk about this again?").

Business Example

Boss agrees. Decision: Grateful ("Thank you for considering this. I appreciate it"). Boss needs approval. Decision: Professional ("When can we revisit this?").

Register Progression

🏠 Casual "Thanks for listening" / "Let's talk again soon"
☕ Neutral "Thank you for considering this"
💼 Professional "I appreciate your time. When can we revisit?"
📋 Formal "Thank you for your consideration. I look forward to our follow-up discussion."
⚖️ Trade-offs
Gain (Graceful) Leaves positive impression, keeps door open.
Lose (Graceful) Takes more time.

Common Mistake

Ending abruptly ("Okay, bye") or over-explaining ("I just want to say that I really appreciate...")

Reflection: Am I closing with professionalism and gratitude, or with abruptness or over-explanation?

3 Common Language Mistakes

Traps that destroy salary conversations

Mistake 1: Being Too Direct

❌ "I want more money."
✅ "I'd like to discuss my compensation."
Why: Direct demands create resistance. Diplomatic language opens dialogue.

Mistake 2: Making Claims Without Evidence

❌ "I think I deserve more because I work hard."
✅ "Based on the impact I've delivered, I believe my compensation should reflect..."
Why: Claims without evidence are easy to dismiss. Facts are harder to ignore.

Mistake 3: Getting Defensive

❌ "But I work harder than others!"
✅ "I understand the constraints. What if we explored alternatives?"
Why: Defensiveness shuts down conversation. Composure keeps it productive.

📖 Case Studies

💼 Case 1: Business - The Performance Review Raise Request

Context: You're at your annual performance review. You've had a strong year: you led a project that generated $500K in revenue, mentored 3 junior team members, and received positive client feedback. You want to ask for a raise.

Conversation Flow:

  • Open: "Thank you for the review. I'd like to discuss my compensation."
  • Support: "Based on the impact I've delivered this year — leading the X project that generated $500K, mentoring 3 team members, and receiving positive client feedback — I believe my compensation should reflect these contributions."
  • Negotiate: "I understand budget constraints. What if we explored alternatives like a performance bonus or equity?"
  • Close: "Thank you for considering this. When can we revisit?"

🎯 LANGUAGE BREAKDOWN (Deep Analysis)

"I'd like to discuss my compensation."
🎯 Language Goal: Open diplomatically. 🧠 Hidden Message: "I'm not demanding. I'm inviting a conversation." ⚖️ Trade-off: Takes 5 seconds, but sets collaborative tone. 🔄 B2 Alternative? "I want to talk about my salary." (Too direct, creates resistance). ⭐ Why This Wins: Diplomatic opening. Shows respect.
"Based on the impact I've delivered..."
🎯 Language Goal: Present value with evidence. 🧠 Hidden Message: "I'm not making claims. I'm presenting facts." ⚖️ Trade-off: Shows confidence, but may sound boastful if not framed carefully. 🔄 B2 Alternative? "I think I deserve more because I work hard." (No evidence, easy to dismiss). ⭐ Why This Wins: Evidence-based. Harder to dismiss.
"What if we explored alternatives?"
🎯 Language Goal: Negotiate collaboratively. 🧠 Hidden Message: "I'm flexible. Let's find a solution together." ⚖️ Trade-off: Shows flexibility, but may feel like you're compromising. 🔄 B2 Alternative? "I need 20% more." (Demanding, creates resistance). ⭐ Why This Wins: Collaborative. Opens dialogue.
"Thank you for considering this. When can we revisit?"
🎯 Language Goal: Close professionally. 🧠 Hidden Message: "I appreciate your time. I'm keeping the door open." ⚖️ Trade-off: Takes more time, but leaves positive impression. 🔄 B2 Alternative? "Okay, thanks." (Abrupt, closes door). ⭐ Why This Wins: Professional. Keeps door open.

🏠 Case 2: Life - Bố Mẹ Hỏi Lương

Context: Bố mẹ hỏi bạn lương bao nhiêu trong bữa cơm gia đình. Bạn muốn trả lời một cách tôn trọng nhưng không muốn chia sẻ chi tiết.

Conversation Flow:

  • Open: "Con hiểu bố mẹ quan tâm."
  • Support: "Con đang ở vị trí tốt về tài chính."
  • Negotiate: "Con thoải mái chia sẻ rằng con đang ở mức X-Y."
  • Close: "Cảm ơn bố mẹ đã quan tâm."

🎯 LANGUAGE BREAKDOWN (Deep Analysis)

"Con hiểu bố mẹ quan tâm."
🎯 Language Goal: Acknowledge their concern. 🧠 Hidden Message: "I respect your concern. I'm not dismissing you." ⚖️ Trade-off: Shows respect, but may feel like you're avoiding. 🔄 B2 Alternative? "Con không muốn nói." (Too direct, creates tension). ⭐ Why This Wins: Respectful. Acknowledges their concern.
"Con đang ở vị trí tốt về tài chính."
🎯 Language Goal: Present value diplomatically. 🧠 Hidden Message: "I'm doing well. You don't need to worry." ⚖️ Trade-off: Reassures them, but doesn't give specifics. 🔄 B2 Alternative? "Con lương cao lắm." (Too casual, may sound boastful). ⭐ Why This Wins: Diplomatic. Reassures without specifics.
"Con thoải mái chia sẻ rằng con đang ở mức X-Y."
🎯 Language Goal: Share diplomatically. 🧠 Hidden Message: "I'm open, but on my terms." ⚖️ Trade-off: Shows openness, but maintains boundaries. 🔄 B2 Alternative? "Con lương X." (Too direct, no context). ⭐ Why This Wins: Diplomatic. Maintains boundaries.

Strategic Grammar in Context

📝 Softening & Diplomacy Grammar

Strategic Goal Language Pattern Example
Diplomatic opening "I'd like to..." / "Would it be possible to..." "I'd like to discuss my compensation."
Softening requests "I was hoping..." / "I was wondering if..." "I was wondering if we could discuss my salary."
Collaborative negotiation "What if we..." / "Would it be possible to..." "What if we explored alternatives?"
Polite pushback "I understand, but..." / "I appreciate, however..." "I understand the constraints, but what if we..."
Professional closing "Thank you for..." / "I appreciate..." "Thank you for considering this."

Pronunciation Focus

🎵 Strategic Pronunciation

1. Diplomatic Tone for Opening
"I'd like to discuss my compensation." (Confident, diplomatic, not demanding)
2. Stress on Evidence
"Based on the impact I've delivered..." (Stress shows preparation)
3. Pause for Impact
"I'm seeking... (pause) ...a 15-20% increase." (Pause lets it sink in)
4. Rising Intonation for Questions
"Would it be possible to consider X? ↗" (Rising = genuine question)

Life

Scenario 1: Bố Mẹ Hỏi Lương

Bố mẹ hỏi lương bao nhiêu trong bữa cơm.
Round 1 (Respectful Opening + Diplomatic Value):
"Con hiểu bố mẹ quan tâm. Con đang ở vị trí tốt về tài chính."
Pushback:
"Thế sao không nói cụ thể?"

"Con hiểu bố mẹ muốn biết chi tiết. Nhưng con thấy thoải mái hơn khi chia sẻ khoảng lương."
Alternative Response:
"Con lương X." (Only if you're comfortable sharing specifics)
Common Mistake: Being too direct ("Con lương X") or too evasive ("Con không muốn nói")
Business

Scenario 2: The Performance Review

You're at your performance review and want to ask for a raise.
Round 1 (Diplomatic Opening + Evidence):
"I'd like to discuss my compensation. Based on the impact I've delivered..."
Pushback:
"Budget is tight right now."
Round 2 (Collaborative Negotiation):
"I understand. What if we explored alternatives? Maybe a performance bonus or equity?"
Alternative Response:
"I need 20% more." (Only if you're willing to risk resistance)
Common Mistake: Being too demanding ("I want more money") or too passive ("Whatever you think is fine")
Life

Scenario 3: Bạn Thân Hỏi Lương

Bạn thân hỏi lương bao nhiêu.
Round 1 (Open + Balanced):
"I'm comfortable sharing that I'm in the X range."
Pushback:
"Thế có đủ sống không?"
Round 2 (Reassuring):
"Đủ sống. Con đang làm việc ở môi trường tốt và có cơ hội phát triển."
Alternative Response:
"I don't want to say." (Only if you're not comfortable sharing)
Common Mistake: Being too direct ("I make X") or too evasive ("I don't want to talk about it")
Business

Scenario 4: Colleague Asks About Your Salary

Đồng nghiệp hỏi lương bao nhiêu.
Round 1 (Diplomatic + Boundary):
"I prefer not to discuss specific numbers, but I'm doing well."
Pushback:
"Why not? We're friends."
Round 2 (Firm but Polite):
"I appreciate your curiosity, but I'm more comfortable keeping that private."
Alternative Response:
"I make X." (Only if you're comfortable sharing)
