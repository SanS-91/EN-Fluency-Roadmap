Teaching Someone a New Skill

Day 97 taught you to give and receive feedback effectively. Day 98 teaches you how to teach someone a new skill — breaking down complex concepts, checking for understanding, and providing constructive feedback. B2 speakers explain what they know. C1 speakers break it down, check understanding, and adapt their teaching style.

Teaching & Learning Day 7 of 7
💡 C1 Insight
C1 Insight
B2 Speaker: Explains what they know in one go. Assumes the learner understands. Doesn't check for understanding or adapt their teaching style.

C1 Speaker: Breaks down complex concepts into digestible chunks. Checks for understanding frequently. Adapts their teaching style based on the learner's needs and feedback.

"B2 speakers teach what they know. C1 speakers teach how the learner learns."
"B2 speakers say 'Let me explain this to you.' C1 speakers say 'Let me break this down step by step — let me know if anything's unclear.' The difference isn't knowledge — it's teaching methodology."

Today's Philosophy

Teaching = Breaking Down + Checking + Adapting

"Teaching someone a new skill isn't about explaining everything you know. It's about breaking down complex concepts, checking for understanding, and adapting your teaching style to the learner's needs."

Every teaching session requires you to make five decisions simultaneously:

Breaking Down Decision: How do I break it down? (All at once? Step by step?)
Checking Decision: How do I check understanding? (Assume? Check frequently?)
Adapting Decision: How do I adapt? (One style? Adapt to learner?)
Feedback Decision: How do I give feedback? (Generic? Specific?)
Pacing Decision: How do I pace? (Too fast? Too slow? Just right?)

Important: Day 98 teaches you how to teach someone a new skill with effective methodology, not just knowledge transfer.

The Teaching Loop

The 3-step process for teaching someone a new skill

1
BREAK DOWN (Break it into chunks)
How do I break it down? "Let me break this down step by step..." / "Let me walk you through this..." (Break into digestible chunks)
2
CHECK (Check for understanding)
How do I check? "Does that make sense?" / "Does that make sense so far?" (Check frequently)
3
ADAPT (Adapt to the learner)
How do I adapt? "Let me try a different approach..." / "Let me explain it differently..." (Adapt to learner's needs)

3 Common Mistakes

Traps that destroy effective teaching

Mistake 1: Explaining All at Once

❌ "Let me explain everything to you at once."
✅ "Let me break this down step by step — let me know if anything's unclear."
Why: Explaining all at once overwhelms the learner. Breaking it down step by step ensures understanding.

Mistake 2: Assuming Understanding

❌ "Do you understand?" (Yes/No question)
✅ "Does that make sense so far? Let me know if anything's unclear."
Why: Yes/No questions don't check understanding. Open questions check understanding effectively.

Mistake 3: Using One Teaching Style

❌ Using the same approach for everyone.
✅ "Let me try explaining it differently — let me know if this makes more sense."
Why: Different learners learn differently. Adapting ensures everyone understands.

Strategic Grammar in Context (5 C1 Patterns)

📝 Strategic Grammar Toolkit (5 C1 Patterns)

Pattern 1: Breaking Down with "Let me break this down step by step"

Used to break down complex concepts. These phrases make complex concepts digestible.
  • "Let me break this down step by step."
  • "Let me break this down into steps."
  • "Let me break this down for you."
  • "Let me break this down step by step — let me know if anything's unclear."
  • "Let me break this down into manageable chunks."
Common Mistake: "Let me explain this to you." (Too vague)
Native Alternative: "Let me break this down step by step."

Pattern 2: Checking with "Does that make sense so far?"

Used to check for understanding. These phrases ensure understanding.
  • "Does that make sense so far?"
  • "Does that make sense?"
  • "Does that make sense to you?"
  • "Does that make sense so far? Let me know if anything's unclear."
  • "Does that make sense? Let me know if you have any questions."
Common Mistake: "Do you understand?" (Yes/No question)
Native Alternative: "Does that make sense so far?"

Pattern 3: Showing with "Let me show you how to"

Used to show how to do something. These phrases show how to do it.
  • "Let me show you how to do this."
  • "Let me show you how to do this step by step."
  • "Let me show you the technique."
  • "Let me show you how to do this — let me know if anything's unclear."
  • "Let me show you how to do this step by step."
Common Mistake: "Let me explain this." (Too vague)
Native Alternative: "Let me show you how to do this."

Pattern 4: Adapting with "Let me try explaining it differently"

Used to adapt to the learner. These phrases adapt to the learner's needs.
  • "Let me try explaining it differently."
  • "Let me try a different approach."
  • "Let me try explaining it differently — let me know if this makes more sense."
  • "Let me try a different approach — let me know if this makes more sense."
  • "Let me try explaining it differently."
Common Mistake: "Let me explain it again." (Too vague)
Native Alternative: "Let me try explaining it differently."

Pattern 5: Encouraging with "You're doing great"

Used to encourage the learner. These phrases encourage the learner.
  • "You're doing great."
  • "You're picking it up really quickly."
  • "You're on the right track."
  • "You're doing great — you're picking it up really quickly."
  • "You're on the right track — let me show you a slightly different approach."
Common Mistake: "Good job." (Too generic)
Native Alternative: "You're doing great."
